Q
What is laser cutting?
A
Laser cutting uses a focused, high-power laser to cut through materials. The beam is controlled for high precision, yet still cuts through thicker materials with ease.
Q
What are the benefits of laser cutting?
A
Laser cutting is faster than traditional machining methods while being just as, if not more, reliable. This allows for short turnaround time on machining that would otherwise be impossible. In certain conditions, laser cutting is also more energy-efficient compared to traditional machining like plasma cutting.
Q
What can laser cutters cut?
A
Laser cutters can cut over 25,000 types of materials. Common uses include cutting metals, such as aluminum and stainless steel, as well as organic materials like wood.
Q
What are TIG and MIG?
A
TIG is Tungsten Inert Gas welding, while MIG is Metal Inert Gas welding. Both methods utilise an electric arc, but have different applications. It’s important to choose the right method for the job.
Q
What is CNC machining?
A
Computer numerical control (CNC) machining makes use of computer programming to write a sequence of instructions for a machine. When executed, the machine will move according to the programming rapidly, efficiently completing a job. CNC is widely applied, including to laser cutting.
